SECTION A: DIEHARD TESTS (test numbers 0-17)
=========================================================================
These are Brown's clean GPL C re-implementations of Marsaglia's original
DIEHARD tests, plus the Marsaglia-Tsang GCD test (number 17).

  0  diehard_birthdays       -- Birthday Spacings
  1  diehard_operm5          -- Overlapping 5-Permutations (OPERM5)
  2  diehard_rank_32x32      -- Binary Rank Test, 32x32 matrices
  3  diehard_rank_6x8        -- Binary Rank Test, 6x8 matrices
  4  diehard_bitstream       -- Bitstream (20-bit missing words)
  5  diehard_opso            -- Overlapping Pairs Sparse Occupancy
  6  diehard_oqso            -- Overlapping Quadruples Sparse Occupancy
  7  diehard_dna             -- DNA (4-letter alphabet sparse occupancy)
  8  diehard_count_1s_stream -- Count the 1's on a stream of bytes
  9  diehard_count_1s_byte   -- Count the 1's on specific bytes
 10  diehard_parking_lot     -- Parking Lot Test
 11  diehard_2dsphere        -- Minimum Distance (2D sphere/circle)
 12  diehard_3dsphere        -- 3D Spheres (minimum distance in 3D)
 13  diehard_squeeze         -- Squeeze Test
 14  diehard_sums            -- Overlapping Sums [MARKED: DO NOT USE -- known buggy]
 15  diehard_runs            -- Runs Test (ascending/descending runs)
 16  diehard_craps           -- Craps Test
 17  marsaglia_tsang_gcd     -- GCD Test (Marsaglia & Tsang, 2002)

Note on test 14 (diehard_sums): This test is marked "Do Not Use" in
dieharder documentation due to implementation issues. It remains in the
suite for historical completeness but should not be used for evaluation.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
TEST 17 (new to dieharder, not in original DIEHARD): marsaglia_tsang_gcd
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Source: Marsaglia and Tsang, "Some Difficult-to-Pass Tests of Randomness,"
Journal of Statistical Software, Vol. 7, Issue 3, 2002.

Generates pairs of random 32-bit unsigned integers (u, v) and computes
two statistics: their greatest common divisor (GCD) and the number of
steps k of Euclid's algorithm required to find it. The distributions of
GCD values and step counts k are compared against the theoretically expected
distributions for truly random 32-bit integers. Tests 10^7 pairs (default).

What it measures: Whether pairs of random integers exhibit the correct GCD
distribution and the correct number of Euclidean algorithm steps. Some
generators produce systematically wrong GCD distributions, exposing
structural correlations between successive output values.

=========================================================================
SECTION B: NIST STS TESTS (test numbers 100-102)
=========================================================================
Brown's re-implementations of three selected tests from NIST SP 800-22.

 100  sts_monobit   -- NIST Monobit (Frequency) Test
 101  sts_runs      -- NIST Runs Test (bit-level transitions)
 102  sts_serial    -- NIST Serial / Generalized Serial Test (n-tuple frequencies)

Note: Dieharder includes only three of NIST SP 800-22's fifteen tests.
Brown considered many of the NIST tests to be weaker than or redundant
with tests in dieharder's other categories (see libdieharder/NOTES).
The libdieharder/NOTES file contains extensive critique of the NIST STS
methodology, particularly of the Runs and Serial statistics.

=========================================================================
SECTION C: RGB / OTHER TESTS (test numbers 200-209)
=========================================================================
These tests are unique to dieharder. Tests 200-204 were written by
Robert G. Brown ("rgb"). Tests 205-209 were contributed by David Bauer
("dab") for dieharder 3.x.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
200  rgb_bitdist -- Bit Distribution Test (RGB original)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tests the distribution of all possible n-bit patterns (n-tuples) within
the generator output. For each bit-width from 1 to n_max (parameterizable,
default up to ~8 bits), slides a window across the bit stream and counts
the frequency of each possible bit pattern. Performs a chi-square test
on the full vector of pattern counts rather than just a single summary
statistic. Brown describes this as a "much better" generalization of both
the NIST Monobit and Runs tests, and as a superior version of Marsaglia's
Bitstream test.

What it measures: Complete bit-pattern frequency distribution at multiple
word lengths simultaneously. Catches any systematic imbalance in the
distribution of 1- through n-bit patterns. Brown notes that essentially
all known weak generators fail this test at 6 bits or fewer.

Note: rgb_bitdist in the source also appears under dh_num_other_tests,
test index 200, and is described in libdieharder/NOTES as "the BEST
generalization of sts_monobit and perhaps better than rgb_binomial as well."

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
201  rgb_minimum_distance -- Generalized Minimum Distance (RGB original)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
A generalization of Marsaglia's 2D and 3D minimum-distance tests extended
to arbitrary dimensionality (parameterizable). Randomly places n points
in a d-dimensional unit hypercube, finds the minimum distance between any
pair of points, and tests whether this minimum distance follows the correct
exponential distribution.

What it measures: Spatial uniformity and independence of random coordinate
tuples in d-dimensional space. More general than DIEHARD's fixed 2D and
3D sphere tests.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
202  rgb_permutations -- Permutations Test (RGB original)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Draws t consecutive values from the generator, determines the rank-ordering
(permutation) of those values, and counts how often each of the t! possible
orderings appears. Performs a chi-square test on the counts against the
uniform distribution over orderings.

What it measures: Whether all orderings of t consecutive output values
appear with equal frequency. Similar in spirit to DIEHARD's OPERM5, but
parameterizable and applied more cleanly.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
203  rgb_lagged_sums -- Lagged Sum Test (RGB original)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Computes sums of uniform deviates sampled from the generator, skipping
a configurable number of samples ("lag") between each value used. Tests
whether these lagged sums are normally distributed with the expected mean
and variance. The lag parameter can be varied to expose autocorrelations
at different distances in the output sequence.

What it measures: Lagged autocorrelations in the generator output. A
generator with period-specific weaknesses (e.g., a linear congruential
generator with certain lags) will produce sums that deviate from expected
normal distributions at specific lag values.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
204  rgb_kstest_test -- Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test Meta-Test (RGB original)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
A meta-test that applies the Kolmogorov-Smirnov (KS) test (specifically
the Kuiper variant, which uses D+ + D- for circular symmetry) to a large
sample of uniform variates from the generator. Verifies that the generator's
output is consistent with a uniform distribution on [0,1) by comparing the
empirical CDF to the theoretical CDF.

What it measures: Overall uniformity of the generator's output distribution.
Unlike tests that examine bit patterns or sequential structure, this directly
tests whether the floating-point values derived from the generator are
uniformly distributed. Uses the Kuiper variant for improved sensitivity
across the full range.

Note: Brown documents that this test has historically produced some false
positives and is considered less reliable than rgb_bitdist for practical
use.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
205  dab_bytedistrib -- Byte Distribution Test (DAB / David Bauer)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tests the frequency distribution of individual byte values (0-255) in the
generator output. Collects byte values from the generator stream and
performs a chi-square test on the 256-bucket histogram to verify that each
possible byte value (0x00 through 0xFF) appears with approximately equal
probability (1/256).

What it measures: Whether the marginal byte-level distribution is uniform.
A simple but sensitive test -- any generator with systematic byte-level
bias will fail this.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
206  dab_dct -- Discrete Cosine Transform Test (DAB / David Bauer)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Applies a discrete cosine transform (DCT) analysis to blocks of generator
output. Detects periodic structure or spectral non-uniformity in the
output sequence that would not be visible in individual-sample distribution
tests.

What it measures: Frequency-domain spectral properties of the generator
output. Can detect periodic patterns or correlations that survive in the
frequency domain even when the time-domain distribution appears uniform.
Ranked second-best in dieharder after dab_monobit2 for detecting bias.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
207  dab_filltree -- Fill Tree Test (DAB / David Bauer)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
Constructs a binary tree (trie) by inserting random bit sequences and
measures how the tree fills up. The depth and fill statistics of the
resulting tree are compared against the expected distributions for a
truly random input.

What it measures: Correlations in the bit-level structure of the generator
output, detected through the structure of a binary trie built from the
output. Correlated generators fill trees differently from independent
random generators.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
208  dab_filltree2 -- Fill Tree Test Variant 2 (DAB / David Bauer)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
A variant of dab_filltree using a different tree construction method or
parameterization to capture different structural correlations.

What it measures: Similar to dab_filltree, tests bit-level correlation
structure via binary tree construction, with different sensitivity
characteristics.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
209  dab_monobit2 -- Enhanced Monobit Test (DAB / David Bauer)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
An enhanced version of the basic monobit (bit-balance) test. Measures the
balance of 0-bits vs. 1-bits at a more granular level than the NIST monobit.
May apply the test at multiple bit positions or over multiple sliding
windows simultaneously to provide higher sensitivity.

What it measures: Bit-level balance (ratio of 0s to 1s) with greater
sensitivity than the basic monobit. Described in testing literature as
"the best test in Dieharder by far" for detecting generator bias, with
dab_dct being the next best.

test registration list (marked as reserved/development in the 900 range):

=========================================================================
SUMMARY: TESTS UNIQUE TO DIEHARDER (not in DIEHARD or NIST SP 800-22)
=========================================================================

From the DIEHARD range (0-99), added beyond original DIEHARD:
  17. marsaglia_tsang_gcd -- GCD and Euclidean steps distribution test
      (from Marsaglia & Tsang 2002 paper, not in original 1995 DIEHARD)

From the STS range (100-102):
  These are all in NIST SP 800-22, so none are unique to dieharder.
  (They are subsets of SP 800-22, not additions to it.)

From the RGB/Other range (200-209) -- ALL unique to dieharder:
  200. rgb_bitdist          -- Multi-width bit-pattern frequency distribution
  201. rgb_minimum_distance -- Generalized N-dimensional minimum distance
  202. rgb_permutations     -- Parameterizable permutation frequency test
  203. rgb_lagged_sums      -- Lagged autocorrelation via sum distributions
  204. rgb_kstest_test      -- KS / Kuiper test of output uniformity
  205. dab_bytedistrib      -- Byte value frequency distribution
  206. dab_dct              -- Discrete Cosine Transform spectral analysis
  207. dab_filltree         -- Binary trie fill statistics
  208. dab_filltree2        -- Binary trie fill statistics (variant 2)
  209. dab_monobit2         -- Enhanced high-sensitivity monobit test

Total: 1 new Marsaglia-family test + 10 entirely original tests = 11 tests
unique to dieharder (not in original DIEHARD 1995 and not in NIST SP 800-22).
